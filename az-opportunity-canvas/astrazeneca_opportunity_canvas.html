02 · Canvas — Clinical Trial Decisioning
Canvas 01: Clinical Trial Decisioning Platform
The problem
Deciding which drug pursuits to advance, hold, or stop is the highest-stakes call in pharma — and it rests on more evidence than any team can hold in view: trial readouts, scientific literature, real-world data, competitive and regulatory signals. ML models score molecules and endpoints but can't reason over unstructured evidence or explain themselves, and portfolio prioritisation happens periodically rather than continuously. The result is slower decisions, late-stage failures, and capital committed to the wrong programmes.

03 · Canvas — Engineering Digital Twin
Canvas 02: Engineering Digital Twin Agents
The problem
The physical engineering behind manufacturing — facility builds, process scale-up, production lines — is capital-intensive, highly regulated (Good Manufacturing Practice — GMP), and unforgiving when it goes wrong. Digital twins of these processes exist, but they are largely passive: they model, they don't govern. Scenario testing and quality control are manual and periodic, so problems surface late and corrections are expensive. The opportunity is agents that act around the twin — simulating scenarios, enforcing control, and catching quality issues before they reach the physical process.

Operational Excellence leadershipAccountable for robustness, throughput and cost of failure
Quality Assurance (QA) / Quality Control (QC) leadershipOwns quality control and the GMP standard the agents must uphold

04 · Canvas — MES Chatbot
Canvas 03: MES Chatbot for GMBR Review
The problem
PAS-X Master Batch Record (MBR) configurations are stored as large, deeply nested XML files. Across design, validation and review cycles, teams spend significant manual effort locating configuration elements, identifying hardcoded values, verifying compliance against Körber guidance, and cross-checking consistency across Generic Master Batch Records (GMBRs). This sits inside a Good Practice (GxP)-regulated environment, so a wrong answer is worse than no answer — and the work is dependent on scarce specialist knowledge of both the XML schema and the PAS-X application. The opportunity is a chatbot that reads the XML accurately, cites every answer at step level, and never fabricates a value that isn't in the file — turning hours of manual inspection into a plain-English conversation.
Stakeholder landscape
Digital Manufacturing LeadOwns the AI initiative and sponsors adoption across the Axial program
Process Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)Verify structural compliance against Körber guidance and internal standards
Quality TeamsCross-check configuration consistency across multiple GMBRs for review
